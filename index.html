"Stick" by Marc Mellits

Comissioned for the 2010 Atlanta Symphony Orchestra Modern Snare Drum Competition. The piece uses 18 seperate extended techniques for the performer, requiring them to play all over the drum. The effect is a composition that seeks to transform the typical monopitched snare drum into a melodic instrument.

"Thunder Song" By Jerod Tate

The Chickasaw word for thunder and lightning is Heloha and, in history, Chickasaw people believed thunderstorms were the beloved, or holy people at war above the clouds. In an attempt to show their bravery and fearlessness of death, warriors would shot their guns into the air during thunder storms. This work is an homage to those old ways. Thunder Song was comissioned by and dedicated to Alex Orfaly.

"Rebonds A" By Iannis Xenakis

Described by Jacques Lonchamps as "An immense abstract ritual, a suite of movements and of hammerings without any folklorist 'contamination', pure music full of marvellously efforescent rhythms, going beyond drama and tempest. A new masterpiece." The work is scored for one percussionist playing only mempranophones (drums with skins): two bass drums, 2 tom-toms, and two bongos."

"Toccata" By Anna Ignatowicz

"Toccata" for Marimba was composed by Anna Ignatowicz in 2001 and is dedicated to her father. It received its debut performance by her husband in Warsaw in 2001. "This is my farewell to my father and a tribute to his memory. When I was writing this work I had to 'go through my daddy's life,' coming back to the times before my birth. A part of myself also changed into ashes at theis moment. If there is any trace of this process in the score, it may only be the utlra-subjective narration, which individual performers may, however, understand in different ways. Toccata is not a programmatic work and my personal comments do not relate to the details of interpretation."
